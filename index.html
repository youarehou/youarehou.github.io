save HOU from TXDOT



Transportation

July 24, 2022

  • Complete TXDOT Survey
  • Leave Voicemail: (833) 933-0446
  • Send Email: UTP-PublicComments@txdot.gov

The folks at the Texas Department of Transportation are inviting public comment on their 10 year Unified Transportation Program. Now, no one takes pride in Houston traffic, pollution, and commute times. And TXDoT wants to help fix this by (*drum roll please*)... adding $7 billion worth of car lanes to I-45.

The folks at Stop TXDoT I45 and Houston Mayor Turner among others are not on board because:

  • (1) Expanding I-45 will force over 1,000 people to leave their homes, schools, churches, and places of work in a manner akin to eminent domain
  • (2) Expanding I-45 will cause more traffic: "traffic engineers tend to think traffic is like a liquid... that’s not true... [traffic] is much more like a gas, meaning the volume of traffic congestion will expand to take up the capacity allowed..."

📬 Register by Mail (easiest)
Ask the nice man in the little circle with two first names a.k.a. Texas Secretary of State John B. Scott to mail you some postage-paid voter registration forms for free. Click link, wait mail, fill out, mail back. Or print your form yourself and drop it off here.


🖥 Update my Registration Online (easiest)
Update your address or name at Texas.gov. You'll need your driver license, social security, or voter ID#.


📍 Register in Person
Pay the folks at Harris County's Elections Department a visit. Print your form, fill it out, and drop it off.
